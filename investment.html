The Basics of Investment

Before you jump into trading and analytics, you need a strong foundation. Understand how to put your money to work so you don't have to work for your money.

swipe to learn more about investing

What Exactly is Investment?

At its core, investing is the act of allocating resources—usually capital (money)—with the expectation of generating an income or profit over time.

Instead of leaving your money in a standard savings account where inflation slowly eats away at its purchasing power, investing allows your capital to grow. You are essentially buying an asset that you believe will increase in value or provide steady returns.

The Power of Compounding

Albert Einstein reportedly called compound interest the "Eighth Wonder of the World."

Compounding happens when the returns on your investments start generating their own returns. Over a 10, 20, or 30-year horizon, this snowball effect is the primary driver of massive wealth generation.

Key Aspects of Investing

Every successful investor must understand these four pillars before putting their hard-earned money into the market.

⚖️

Risk vs. Return

The fundamental rule of markets: higher potential returns usually come with higher risk. Government bonds offer low risk and low returns, while early-stage tech stocks offer high risk but potentially massive rewards.

🧺

Diversification

"Don't put all your eggs in one basket." By spreading your investments across different asset classes (stocks, real estate, gold), you protect your portfolio against the failure of a single company or sector.

⏳

Time Horizon

Your investment strategy heavily depends on when you need the money. Money needed in 2 years shouldn't be in highly volatile stocks, while a retirement fund 30 years away should aggressively seek growth.

🎯

Investment Goals

Defining objectives such as wealth creation, retirement planning, or funding specific needs determines the strategy.

📈

Compounding

The process where investment returns generate their own earnings, favoring early and long-term investors.

💧

Liquidity

Liquidity refers to how quickly you can turn an asset back into cash. Stocks are highly liquid (can be sold in seconds), while real estate is illiquid (takes months to sell).
